About

Claude Oscar Monet

The couple Adolphe and Louise Justine Monet gave birth to the world's most influential painter on November 14, 1840 in Paris. The parents' biggest dream for Monet was to continue the family business. However, all that changed when Monet entered Le Havre school in April 1851. Monet became acquainted with the world of drawing and organized the first caricature exhibition on the beaches of Normandy. There, he met Boudin who became a mentor for using oil paints and the en plein air technique.

Monet later joined Charles Gleyre's studio in Paris. He did not continue his studies at the University because he felt a conflict with the classical lessons taught. Later, it was here that Monet met Pierre Auguste Renoir, Frederic Bazille and Alfred Sisley. Together they developed a new technique that we know as Impressionism. This technique is based on the effects of light reflections that are captured spontaneously by the human eye.

In 1923, Claude Monet had to have two operations due to cataracts. Post-surgery effects give a different color to his painting style. His work turns into a red tonality, a very natural reaction for cataract sufferers. In addition to the red tonality, Monet can sometimes detect the reflection of ultraviolet light. After the operation, Monet often repainted his previous works. Monet died on December 5, 1926 at the age of 86, and was buried in the Giverny church cemetery. During the 86 years of his life, Claude Monet has produced many paintings.
